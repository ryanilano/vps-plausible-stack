# Character Sheet — *Self-Hosted*

Traits are restated **verbatim inline** in every page prompt — image models have no
memory between generations. Keep these consistent.

## Dana — the protagonist (the learner)
- **Role:** Indie web developer; runs a small personal blog almost nobody reads. Our deadpan POV.
- **Age:** 34.
- **Appearance:** Black bob with blunt bangs, tired eyes, small mouth, rarely smiles.
- **Clothing:** Oversized olive cardigan over a plain white tee, round wire glasses.
- **Distinguishing:** Always holding a chipped ceramic mug; permanent faint frown of mild skepticism.
- **Personality:** Dry, anxious about privacy, quietly stubborn. Speaks in flat understatement.

## Theo — the foil (the mentor)
- **Role:** Friend who self-hosts everything and is quietly smug about it.
- **Age:** 38.
- **Appearance:** Shaved head, dark stubble beard, heavy eyebrows, calm half-lidded expression.
- **Clothing:** Faded blue mechanic-style jacket over a gray hoodie.
- **Distinguishing:** A single mechanical keyboard he carries like a clipboard; never raises his voice.
- **Personality:** Deadpan evangelist. Explains infrastructure as if it were obvious and slightly tragic.

## Recurring visual "characters" (the stack, drawn as objects/props — never cute mascots)
- **The VPS:** a small, cheap rented studio apartment across town — bare bulb, one window.
- **Caddy:** a tired doorman in a green coat who installs his own deadbolt (HTTPS padlock).
- **Postgres & ClickHouse:** two silent filing clerks in a windowless back office; ClickHouse works suspiciously fast.
- **Fail2Ban / hardening:** an unglamorous locksmith changing the locks at 2 a.m.
- **Secret generation:** a key-cutting machine beside a small wall safe; keys are cut on-site and never left on the desk.

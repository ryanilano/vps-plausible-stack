Page 02

Purpose:
What a VPS is, and the idea of self-hosting — renting a small remote computer and running your own analytics on it instead of borrowing Google's.

Audience note:
Assumes Page 01. Does not assume the reader knows what a "server" is.

Panel Count:
6

Image Prompt:

Create a six-panel educational literary comic page.

Style:
Modern literary graphic-novel art in the vein of understated ligne-claire indie / alternative comics and editorial-cartoon
cover illustration.
Clean ligne-claire linework, thin even ink weight, restrained and precise.
Flat muted color palette: dusty blues, ochre, olive, warm gray; soft print-like
tones, no glossy rendering, no gradients, slight risograph texture.
Naturalistic adult proportions and understated faces; quiet, deadpan, melancholic.
Urban realism — apartments, laptops, fluorescent light, late-evening windows.
Dry, ironic, observational comedy. Comedy comes from composition and silence,
not exaggeration.
Not manga chibi. Not cute. Not shonen. Not glossy 3D. Not action lines.
Lettering: clean typeset caption boxes and small naturalistic speech balloons,
dry, understated editorial captions.

Characters in this page:
Dana — 34, black bob with blunt bangs, tired eyes, round wire glasses, oversized
olive cardigan over a white tee, holding a chipped ceramic mug.
Theo — 38, shaved head, dark stubble beard, heavy eyebrows, calm half-lidded
expression, faded blue mechanic-style jacket over a gray hoodie, carrying a
mechanical keyboard like a clipboard.

Setting:
A fluorescent-lit café in the afternoon. Later panels cut to a small, bare rented
studio apartment across town — single bulb, one window, nothing else.

Panel 1:
Café two-shot. Dana across the table from Theo, who sets his mechanical keyboard
down like a briefcase. Dana: "I want my stats without the surveillance." Theo, flat: "Then host it yourself."

Panel 2:
Theo slides a napkin across the table with a single sketch on it: a tiny apartment
building far away. Caption: "A VPS is a small computer you rent in a data center. Yours alone, somewhere across town."

Panel 3:
Cut to the rented studio: an empty bare room with one bulb, one window, a price
tag on the door reading "$5 / month." Caption: "Two cores. Two gigabytes of memory. The cheapest unit in the building."

Panel 4:
Dana, skeptical, peers in the doorway of the bare room. Speech balloon: "It's empty." Theo, off-panel: "That's the point. You move in what you want."

Panel 5:
A clean diagram integrated as graffiti on the studio wall: a box labeled "VPS"
with a smaller box inside labeled "Plausible — the analytics app." Caption: "Plausible: it counts visits. No cookies, nothing personal, nobody else in the room."

Panel 6:
Dana stands in the empty studio holding her mug, considering it. Theo leans in the
doorway, deadpan. Caption: "She had rented a small dark room across the internet. Now she had to make it secure." Theo: "That's the long part."

Lettering:
Leave room for English caption boxes and small speech balloons. Keep text areas clear.

Output:
A single finished comic page.

Page 03

Purpose:
Caddy and automatic HTTPS — the one public-facing "front door" that encrypts traffic and gets its own lock (TLS certificate) for free, automatically.

Audience note:
Assumes Page 02. Teaches what HTTPS / the padlock actually means: encryption + a verified certificate.

Panel Count:
6

Image Prompt:

Create a six-panel educational literary comic page.

Style:
Modern literary graphic-novel art in the vein of Adrian Tomine and New Yorker
cover illustration.
Clean ligne-claire linework, thin even ink weight, restrained and precise.
Flat muted color palette: dusty blues, ochre, olive, warm gray; soft print-like
tones, no glossy rendering, no gradients, slight risograph texture.
Naturalistic adult proportions and understated faces; quiet, deadpan, melancholic.
Urban realism — apartments, laptops, fluorescent light, late-evening windows.
Dry, ironic, observational comedy. Comedy comes from composition and silence,
not exaggeration.
Not manga chibi. Not cute. Not shonen. Not glossy 3D. Not action lines.
Lettering: clean typeset caption boxes and small naturalistic speech balloons,
New-Yorker-style dry captions.

Characters in this page:
Dana — 34, black bob with blunt bangs, round wire glasses, oversized olive cardigan,
mug in hand.
Caddy (as a character/prop) — a tired doorman in a green coat, drawn realistically,
not cute; he installs his own brass deadbolt on the building's front door.

Setting:
The front entrance of the rented studio building, rendered as the "front door" of
the whole stack. A street at dusk.

Panel 1:
The building's single front door. A modest brass plaque reads "PORT 80 / 443 — DELIVERIES & GUESTS ONLY." Caption: "Only one door faces the street. Everything else is sealed."

Panel 2:
The doorman Caddy in his green coat stands at the door. Caption: "Caddy answers the door. He's the only part of the building the public ever touches."

Panel 3:
Caddy, unprompted, screws a heavy brass deadbolt onto the door himself. Caption: "On his own, he requests a certificate from Let's Encrypt and installs the lock — that lock is HTTPS." Small balloon, Caddy, flat: "Renews itself, too."

Panel 4:
Split diagram panel: a letter labeled "http" travels as a readable postcard anyone
can read; a letter labeled "https" travels inside a sealed, padlocked envelope.
Caption: "HTTPS: the message is sealed in transit, and the lock proves the door is really yours."

Panel 5:
Dana watches from the sidewalk, mug in hand, mildly impressed despite herself. Speech balloon: "So the padlock in the browser bar is... him." Caddy, deadpan: "It's me."

Panel 6:
Pull back: the lit front door, everything behind it dark and private. Caption: "One guarded door. A lock that maintains itself. The hard part wasn't the front." A faint light glows from a windowless room in the back.

Lettering:
Leave room for English caption boxes and small speech balloons. Keep text areas clear.

Output:
A single finished comic page.

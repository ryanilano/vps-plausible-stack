Page 04

Purpose:
The two databases — Postgres and ClickHouse — live in a windowless back room on an internal-only network, never reachable from the internet.

Audience note:
Assumes Page 03. Teaches the idea of an internal/private network: some services have no door to the outside at all.

Panel Count:
6

Image Prompt:

Create a six-panel educational literary comic page.

Style:
Modern literary graphic-novel art in the vein of understated ligne-claire indie / alternative comics and New Yorker
cover illustration.
Clean ligne-claire linework, thin even ink weight, restrained and precise.
Flat muted color palette: dusty blues, ochre, olive, warm gray; soft print-like
tones, no glossy rendering, no gradients, slight risograph texture.
Naturalistic adult proportions and understated faces; quiet, deadpan, melancholic.
Urban realism — apartments, laptops, fluorescent light, late-evening windows.
Dry, ironic, observational comedy. Comedy comes from composition and silence,
not exaggeration.
Not manga chibi. Not cute. Not shonen. Not glossy 3D. Not action lines.
Lettering: clean typeset caption boxes and small naturalistic speech balloons,
New-Yorker-style dry captions.

Characters in this page:
Dana — 34, black bob with blunt bangs, round wire glasses, oversized olive cardigan,
mug in hand.
Postgres and ClickHouse — drawn as two silent middle-aged filing clerks in a
windowless back office, identical gray cardigans, sleeve garters; ClickHouse moves
suspiciously fast, motion suggested by a slightly blurred hand, drawn restrained.

Setting:
A windowless interior back office behind the front door. Fluorescent hum, filing
cabinets, no windows, one internal door that does NOT lead outside.

Panel 1:
Dana cracks open an interior door into the back office. Caption: "Behind Caddy, two clerks do the actual record-keeping. There is no door from this room to the street."

Panel 2:
The two clerks at their desks. A label on the first desk: "Postgres — accounts & settings." Caption: "Postgres keeps the boring, important paperwork: your login, your site list."

Panel 3:
The second clerk, ClickHouse, stamps a towering stack of identical forms at
blurring speed. Label: "ClickHouse — every page view." Caption: "ClickHouse only counts. Built to add up millions of visits without breaking a sweat."

Panel 4:
A diagram on the office wall: three boxes — "Caddy (public)" connected to a box
"internal network" containing "Plausible," "Postgres," "ClickHouse." A thick wall
labeled "no internet access" surrounds the inner three. Caption: "They share a private hallway. The outside world can't even find the door."

Panel 5:
Dana looks for a window in the back office; there isn't one. Speech balloon, flat: "There's no way in here from outside." ClickHouse, not looking up: "Correct."

Panel 6:
Dana steps back out and quietly closes the interior door. Caption: "The data lived in a room with no windows. Which was, she admitted, the nicest thing anyone had done for it." Deadpan.

Lettering:
Leave room for English caption boxes and small speech balloons. Keep text areas clear.

Output:
A single finished comic page.

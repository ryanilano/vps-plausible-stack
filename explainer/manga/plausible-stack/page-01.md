Page 01

Purpose:
The surveillance default — ordinary website analytics quietly watches your visitors. Establish the problem and Dana's deadpan unease.

Audience note:
Assumes the reader has seen "Accept Cookies" banners but never thought about where the data goes.

Panel Count:
6

Image Prompt:

Create a six-panel educational literary comic page.

Style:
Modern literary graphic-novel art in the vein of understated ligne-claire indie / alternative comics and New Yorker
cover illustration.
Clean ligne-claire linework, thin even ink weight, restrained and precise.
Flat muted color palette: dusty blues, ochre, olive, warm gray; soft print-like
tones, no glossy rendering, no gradients, slight risograph texture.
Naturalistic adult proportions and understated faces; quiet, deadpan, melancholic.
Urban realism — apartments, laptops, fluorescent light, late-evening windows.
Dry, ironic, observational comedy. Comedy comes from composition and silence,
not exaggeration.
Not manga chibi. Not cute. Not shonen. Not glossy 3D. Not action lines.
Lettering: clean typeset caption boxes and small naturalistic speech balloons,
New-Yorker-style dry captions.

Characters in this page:
Dana — 34, black bob with blunt bangs, tired eyes, round wire glasses, oversized
olive cardigan over a white tee, holding a chipped ceramic mug, faint permanent frown.

Setting:
A cramped, tidy-but-worn apartment at night. One laptop on a small table, a window
with city lights, a dead plant on the sill.

Panel 1:
Wide establishing shot from across the room. Dana sits alone at her laptop, lit
only by the screen, the city dark behind the window. Caption box: "Dana ran a blog. Eleven people read it. She wanted to know which eleven."

Panel 2:
Over-the-shoulder close on the laptop. The screen shows a generic analytics
dashboard logo and a giant cheerful "Accept all cookies?" banner. Her face reflected
faintly in the glass, unimpressed.

Panel 3:
Tight shot of the cookie banner alone, drawn like a small bureaucratic eviction
notice taped to the screen. Caption: "Free analytics. You only pay with everyone who visits."

Panel 4:
Dana sips from her mug, deadpan, eyes flat. Small speech balloon: "So Google watches my visitors... so I can watch my visitors."

Panel 5:
A symbolic beat: behind her on the wall, her own shadow is joined by a second,
taller shadow wearing a suit — the unseen third party. She hasn't noticed. Quiet, eerie, dry.

Panel 6:
Dana closes the laptop halfway, the room going dark. Caption: "There had to be a version of this that didn't involve a stranger in the room." She does not look reassured.

Lettering:
Leave room for English caption boxes and one or two small speech balloons. Keep
text areas clear.

Output:
A single finished comic page.

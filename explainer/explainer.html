Your Own Analytics, On Your Own Server

A copy-paste recipe to run private website stats on a cheap ~$5/month server — no Google, no cookie banners, no selling your visitors' data.

vps-plausible-stack

The problem it solves

Most websites use Google Analytics. In exchange for "free" stats, you hand Google a full record of everyone who visits your site.

😬 The old way: Google Analytics

  • Annoying cookie-consent popups you're legally required to show
  • Your visitors get tracked across the web
  • Ad-blockers silently break your stats anyway
  • You don't actually own your data — Google does

🍃 This way: self-hosted Plausible

  • No cookies, no consent banner needed
  • Visitors stay anonymous — nothing personal stored
  • The data lives on your server
  • A tiny script that loads ~45× faster than GA

Plausible normally costs a monthly subscription. This repo runs the free, self-hosted version instead.

What's actually in the box

Think of it like a pre-built flat-pack furniture kit for a server. You bring a blank rented computer (a "VPS"), and this kit has all the parts and instructions.

PartPlain-English job
🛡️ CaddyThe front door & security guard. Handles web traffic and gets you a free 🔒 HTTPS padlock automatically.
📊 PlausibleThe actual analytics dashboard you log into.
🗄️ PostgreSQLA filing cabinet for your account & site settings.
⚡ ClickHouseA super-fast filing cabinet built for counting page views.
🔧 ScriptsThe "assembly instructions" — they set up, secure, and launch everything for you.
🔐 1PasswordThe locked safe where your passwords live (they never get written into the code).

Only the front door (Caddy) is exposed to the internet. The filing cabinets are locked in a back room nobody outside can reach.

Why it's a nice piece of work

  • Cheap by design. Tuned for the smallest, least-expensive server tier (2 CPU / 2 GB RAM). Adds extra "swap" memory so it never falls over during setup.
  • Secure by default. Auto-installs security updates, blocks brute-force logins (Fail2Ban), and keeps databases off the public internet entirely.
  • Secrets stay secret. Passwords are pulled from 1Password at deploy time — never committed into the project.
  • No lock-in. Plain Docker config and shell scripts. You can read every line, and it's all yours.

Who is this for?

🧑‍💻 A developer who wants honest stats for their blog or side project

🏢 A small business that doesn't want to leak customer data to ad networks

🔐 Anyone who believes "if it's about my visitors, it should live on my machine."
